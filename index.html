01 · THE ARC
One motion, one commitment

A single arm sweeps a full 180° from a low pickup pose to a high scoring pose. No lift column, no telescoping stages — the arm stays compact against the chassis, which keeps the robot balanced and predictable.

02 · THE GRAB
Two stacks of two

The claw, tuned with rubber bands for grip, picks up Pin stacks off the floor. The 180 Mech is designed to hold two stacks of two Pins at once — double the payload per cycle, double the score per trip.

03 · THE 110 STACK
Onto the Standoff

The finished piece goes onto the elevated Standoff Goal — a single pin at the base, the beam across the top, and two more pins slotted into each side of the beam. Elevated scoring, high point value, won by robots that can actually reach up and place clean.

The 180 Mech is simple to build and honest about its tradeoffs — the arm arc means every driving decision commits the whole robot to a direction. That's why we chose it: once you learn it, you stop fighting it. Top teams running this design in Mix & Match have posted scores in the 180+ point range. That's the ceiling we're climbing.
